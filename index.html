Colorado Railroads and Locomotives

By Declan Henry

Colorado has a rich railroad history. There were many narrow gauge and standard gauge railroads throught the state. Many remnants of these railroads survive, ranging from small artifacts to complete working railroad sections (See the Durango and Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad).

Many of the railroads in Colorado were narrow gauge, which means that the rails are closer together. This allowed for all the infrastructure needed for the track to be smaller as well. The advantage to this was being able to lay track in the mountainous terrain of the state. Constructing a full sized railroad in this setting would be time consuming and expensive. Many of the railroads were mining railroads, so this was necessary.
